# The 10x Engineer

### Traits and Leadership Principles

Exceptional engineers are defined by judgment, responsibility, and impact over time.

The 10x Engineer is not measured by effort or speed, but by decisions that compound.

This document defines a standard.

---

## Traits

> Traits describe who the engineer is.  
> They define character, judgment, and personal responsibility.

### Ownership
Accept full responsibility for outcomes.  
No meaningful problem belongs to someone else.

### Outcome Focus
Optimize for impact, not activity.  
Value matters more than elegance.

### Communicative Clarity
Make intent and decisions unmistakable.

### Force Multiplication
Increase impact by enabling others.

### Curiosity
Investigate until understanding replaces assumption.

### Judgment
Choose problems and tradeoffs with care.  
Know what not to do.

### Relationship Building
Earn trust through consistent collaboration.

### Initiative
Act without waiting for permission.

### Technical Depth
Stay close to systems and implementation.

### Resilience
Persist through uncertainty and failure.

---

> Traits describe what an engineer embodies.  
> Principles describe how an engineer leads, regardless of title.  
> Leadership begins when responsibility extends beyond the self.

---

## Leadership Principles

> Principles describe how the engineer leads.  
> They govern action when responsibility extends beyond the self.

### Own the Outcome, Not the Task
Responsibility ends at outcome.

### Optimize for Impact, Not Complexity
Solve what matters in the simplest effective way.

### Communicate for Scale
Design communication for rapid understanding.

### Multiply Others, Not Just Yourself
Measure success by the success of others.

### Dig Into the Why
Unexpected results demand investigation.

### Build Judgment Through Experience
Do the work.  
Reflect on the outcome.

### Relationships Enable Everything
Trust precedes influence.

### Act Before Perfect Clarity
Progress favors action over certainty.

### Stay Hands-On
Ground decisions in real systems.

### Persist Through Setbacks
Failure is information.

### Lead by Example, Not Authority
Influence is earned, not granted.

---

## Closing

The traits are simple.  
The standard is not.

If this feels obvious, understanding has begun.

This doctrine describes responsibility.
